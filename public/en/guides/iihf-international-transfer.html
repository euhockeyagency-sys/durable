IIHF international transfer: how a move works

A practical guide to an IIHF international player transfer between national associations, including limited and unlimited transfers and club responsibilities.

When an international transfer is used

When a player's playing rights move from one national association to another, the IIHF international process applies. The new club and receiving association should start and confirm the process under the current regulations.

Limited and unlimited transfers

IIHF regulations distinguish limited and unlimited transfers. A limited transfer places playing rights with a specific receiving association for an agreed duration. An unlimited transfer moves those rights without that time limit. The choice should match the contract and the player's intentions.

Typical sequence

  1. the player and new club agree terms;
  2. the club provides details to the receiving association;
  3. the request is submitted through the IIHF system;
  4. the current or home association reviews it;
  5. after approval, the club completes national registration.

The club and associations confirm exact documents, fees and timing.

What the transfer does not solve

IIHF approval does not replace compliance with an existing contract, national registration, a visa, work authorisation, insurance or age eligibility.

Questions for the club

  • transfer type and duration;
  • which association currently holds the playing rights;
  • who pays the fees;
  • the submission deadline;
  • written confirmation that registration is complete.

FAQ

Is an international transfer always required?

It is normally used when playing rights move between national associations; the associations confirm the individual case.

How does limited differ from unlimited?

A limited transfer applies to a specific association and duration; an unlimited transfer moves the playing rights without that duration.

Does an IIHF transfer provide work permission?

No. Immigration and employment status are separate checks.
