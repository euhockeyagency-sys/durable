Hockey agent in Germany: club search and market assessment

A guide to working with the German hockey market. EHA does not claim a physical office in Germany; the service is market assessment, player preparation and targeted club outreach.

What “hockey agent in Germany” should mean

For a player, the key issue is not the agent's address but whether the German league pyramid, club needs and administrative route are understood. Representation does not replace the club's decision. It helps build a verifiable profile, identify a realistic level and manage communication consistently.

Who may fit the German market

Germany has several senior levels, from DEL and DEL2 to Oberliga, plus a developed junior system. A large number of teams does not mean an open roster spot. Position, age, current league, role, production, video, citizenship and contract status must be assessed together.

See the Hockey in Germany country guide for the league structure and official links.

Checks before clubs are contacted

  1. whether the player's pace and role fit the target level;
  2. whether Elite Prospects, statistics and video are current;
  3. whether the player is contractually free and where the playing rights sit;
  4. how citizenship affects registration and work status;
  5. whether a club has a relevant positional need.

How the process works

The profile is assessed first and a short list of realistic levels is selected. The resume and video are then corrected, a clear club presentation is prepared and targeted communication begins. A reply, tryout or document request is an intermediate step rather than a contract promise.

What to submit

  • Elite Prospects and recent-season statistics;
  • recent highlights and a full game;
  • position, shot, height, weight and date of birth;
  • citizenship and available passports;
  • current contract, playing rights and availability date;
  • target level and expectations.

Documents and timing

An international transfer, national registration and the right to work are separate procedures. For third-country nationals, the club and player should identify the appropriate immigration route in advance. See the visas and transfers section.

FAQ

Can an agent guarantee a contract in Germany?

No. Only a club can offer a contract after sporting and administrative assessment.

Is an EU passport required?

Not always, but citizenship affects work rights and the administrative attractiveness of a profile.

When should the search begin?

Prepare materials early. Club activity often increases in spring and summer, but exact timing depends on level and season.
