Hockey in Poland: leagues, imports and work rules

A practical guide for players. League rules, level and immigration requirements must be checked for the relevant season and individual case.

01

League structure

EHA's reviewed league reference lists two senior levels in Poland:

  • PHL / TAURON Hokej Liga — the top division.
  • 1 liga — the second level and a separate development or adaptation route.

The Polish federation publishes junior competitions, including Junior CLJ and age-group categories. Competition names and groups can change, so use the federation's current-season page.

02

Comparing the levels

Level matters more than the country's name. A strong league on a statistics page does not by itself prove readiness for a specific role. Clubs compare pace, age, position, responsibilities and the quality of the available video.

03

Where imports are considered

Both Polish senior levels are marked as comparatively open to imports in `leagues.src.js`. This is a search guideline, not a promise of an opening. Actual access depends on the season's rules, roster, club budget and whether the player occupies an import spot.

PHLOpenness: high

A club will expect an import to improve a clearly defined role.

1 ligaOpenness: high

It can be a route into the market when the level and conditions match.

04

Age and citizenship

For younger players, game opportunities, development potential and age eligibility matter. For senior players, clubs need a proven role, consistent production and the ability to contribute now. Citizenship affects paperwork and import status, but it does not replace sporting evaluation.

05

EU and non-EU passports

Citizenship of an EU member state generally provides easier labour-market access than third-country citizenship. It does not guarantee a roster spot or remove hockey registration requirements. Before committing to a non-EU player, the club should confirm a valid immigration and employment route.

Important: import status under sporting regulations and the legal right to work are separate questions. Both must be checked.
06

Transfer, registration, visa and work

  1. Transfer: a move between national associations uses the IIHF International Transfer Card process under the current regulations.
  2. Registration: the receiving club confirms the PZHL requirements and the rules of the specific competition.
  3. Stay: a third-country national needs a lawful immigration status that permits the intended purpose.
  4. Work: the right to work depends on citizenship, immigration status, the contract and any applicable permit or exemption.

This is general orientation, not legal advice. Before signing, verify the current-season requirements with the club and PZHL, and confirm immigration rules through Poland's official portal and the competent consulate.

07

When clubs build their rosters

The main roster-building window is April to August. Targeted openings may appear later because of injuries, budget changes or roster performance. The right time to approach a club is when current statistics, a clear profile and recent video are ready.

08

Statistics and video clubs need

  • an Elite Prospects link and verifiable season statistics;
  • current club, league, role, special-teams usage and ice time where verifiable;
  • short, recent video with clear player identification;
  • full shifts or games when requested;
  • contact details, passport status and a realistic availability date.

Detailed Russian resources cover a hockey resume and club video. English-speaking players can submit the same materials directly for review.

09

EHA cases

EHA places players across European markets and only publishes examples that can be supported without inventing a country-specific connection. Review the agency's general cases. A previous case is not a guarantee of the same outcome for another player.

Is an international transfer required?

A move between national associations normally uses the IIHF process. The club and PZHL should confirm the exact steps.

Does an EU passport guarantee a spot?

No. It affects administration, while the sporting decision remains with the club.

When should I send my profile?

The main window is April to August; individual openings can also appear during the season.
