Hockey in Finland: leagues, imports and work rules

A practical orientation for players. League rules, roster status and immigration requirements must be checked for the current season and the individual case.

01

League structure

The EHA league reference lists three senior levels in Finland:

  • Liiga — the elite top division.
  • Mestis — the professional second level.
  • Suomi-sarja — the third senior level and a possible entry market for the right profile.

Finland also operates national U20 and U18 competitions with several levels. Names, groups and age eligibility must be checked in the Finnish Ice Hockey Association’s current competition service.

02

Comparing levels

LiigaElite professional level
MestisProfessional second level
Suomi-sarjaNational third level

Level matters more than the country name. Finnish hockey values pace, decision-making and role execution. A club compares the player’s current competition, age, position, video and expected role.

03

Where imports are realistically considered

In the EHA league data, Liiga is marked as difficult for imports, while Mestis and Suomi-sarja are marked as limited rather than closed. This is a market orientation, not proof of a vacancy.

LiigaHard

Only players who are clear upgrades for a defined role are realistic candidates.

MestisLimited

The sporting threshold remains high, but the market is broader than Liiga.

Suomi-sarjaLimited

Can be an entry route when level, role, status and club budget align.

04

Age and citizenship

For juniors, the club evaluates development value, school and housing arrangements, age eligibility and expected ice time. For senior players, the focus shifts to current production, role, physical readiness and immediate impact. Citizenship affects administration but does not replace sporting assessment.

05

EU versus non-EU passport

An EU/EEA citizen normally has a simpler right-to-work route than a third-country national. A non-EU player must confirm the correct residence and employment basis with the club before travel or registration.

Important: federation registration, an import designation and the legal right to work are separate questions.
06

Transfer, registration, visa and work

  1. Transfer: a move between national associations normally uses the current IIHF international transfer process.
  2. Registration: the receiving club confirms Finnish federation and league requirements.
  3. Residence: a third-country national needs a lawful residence basis for the intended activity.
  4. Work: Migri has a dedicated residence-permit route for professional athletes, coaches and referees; eligibility depends on the contract and current requirements.

This is general information, not legal advice. Check the current season with the club and Finnish Ice Hockey Association, and immigration requirements with Migri or the competent Finnish mission.

07

When clubs build rosters

The main outreach window is generally April to August. Individual openings may appear later because of injuries, budget changes or roster adjustments. A current profile and recent full-game video should be ready before outreach begins.

08

Stats and video clubs need

  • current Elite Prospects and verifiable season statistics;
  • league, team role, special-teams usage and availability;
  • a short recent highlight video with clear player identification;
  • full-game or full-shift footage;
  • citizenship, contract status and realistic target level.

Prepare a clear player resume and video package before approaching clubs.

09

EHA cases

EHA places players across European markets and publishes only cases that can be shown honestly. Review the general EHA cases. A previous transfer is not a guarantee of the same result for another player.

Is an international transfer normally required?

A move between national associations normally follows the IIHF process. The receiving club and federation confirm the exact steps.

Does an EU passport guarantee a roster spot?

No. It may simplify administration, but the club still decides on sporting fit.

When should a player submit a profile?

The main window is April to August, with individual opportunities possible during the season.
