Hockey in Germany: leagues, imports and work rules

A practical orientation for players. Re-check league, registration and immigration rules for the current season and individual case.

01

League structure

The EHA league reference currently lists the following senior levels for Germany:

  • DEL — top professional division
  • DEL2 — professional second level
  • Oberliga — third level with North and South groups

The main national junior route includes DNL and federation age-group competitions. Check the current season format and eligibility.

02

Comparing levels

Level matters more than the country name. Clubs compare pace, role, age, production, video and the quality of the player’s previous competition.

03

The labels below come from the EHA league reference and describe general market openness. They do not prove a current vacancy or a guaranteed import place.

04

Age and citizenship

Junior players are assessed on development potential, age eligibility, expected ice time, education and housing. Senior players must show a role they can perform immediately. Citizenship affects registration and employment administration, but not the sporting standard.

05

EU versus non-EU passport

An EU/EEA passport can simplify the employment route in EU/EEA markets. A third-country national usually needs additional residence or work formalities. The club must confirm the route before travel and registration.

Important: import status under sporting rules and the legal right to work are separate checks.
06

Transfer, registration, visa and work

  1. Transfer: an international move normally follows the current IIHF transfer process.
  2. Registration: the receiving club confirms federation and competition requirements.
  3. Residence: non-EU/EEA players need a lawful basis for their intended stay.
  4. Work: the contract and immigration status must permit the proposed sporting activity.

The federal Make it in Germany portal explains visa and employment routes; the correct basis depends on the contract, citizenship and activity.

This is general orientation, not legal advice. Check the current season with the club and federation, and immigration rules with the official authority or competent consulate.

07

When clubs build rosters

The main roster-building window is generally April to August. Individual needs may appear later because of injuries, budget changes or roster corrections. Prepare the profile before the market becomes active.

08

Stats and video clubs need

  • current Elite Prospects and verifiable statistics;
  • team, league, role, special-teams usage and availability;
  • a short recent highlight video with clear identification;
  • full-game or full-shift footage;
  • citizenship, contract status and realistic target level.

A club should be able to understand the player’s level and role quickly.

09

EHA cases

EHA places players across European markets and publishes only cases that can be shown honestly. Review the general EHA cases. A previous transfer is not a guarantee for another player.

Is an international transfer normally required?

A move between national associations normally follows the IIHF process. The receiving club and federation confirm the exact steps.

Does an EU passport guarantee a place?

No. It may change the administrative route, but the sporting decision remains with the club.

When should a player submit a profile?

The main window is April to August; individual opportunities can appear during the season.
